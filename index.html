    About the Scanlon Leadership Archive

    Preserving over 80 years of people-centered leadership thought, practice, and transformation.

    Our Mission

    The Scanlon Leadership Archive is a collaborative digital space dedicated to preserving, organizing, and sharing the rich legacy of the Scanlon Leadership Network. Our mission is to make the wisdom of people-centered, servant leadership accessible to leaders, researchers, students, and change agents around the world.

    The Scanlon Story

    Joseph N. Scanlon (1897‚Äì1956) was a steelworker, cost accountant, union leader, and eventually a lecturer at MIT. During the Great Depression, Scanlon discovered that when workers were trusted, informed, and given a voice, extraordinary results followed.

    His work was carried forward by Dr. Carl Frost at Michigan State University, who developed the four Frost/Scanlon Principles ‚Äî Identity, Participation, Equity, and Competence. Frost's clients, including Herman Miller and Donnelly Corporation, became some of the most admired workplaces in America.

    The EPIC Principles

    Identity ‚Äî The Foundation of Performance

    Who are we? What do we value? How are we unique? Identity encompasses the purpose and values of the individual, the team, and the total organization.

    Participation ‚Äî Shared Responsibility

    The ability to influence decisions in your area of competence. When groups solve problems together, synergy develops.

    Equity ‚Äî Stakeholder Accountability

    Treating all stakeholders fairly. Employees need safety and fair wages. Customers need quality at fair prices.

    Competence ‚Äî Continuous Growth

    As people become more competent, they practice the other three principles better. Competence ties everything together.
